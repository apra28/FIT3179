USA Cities Compared - 2015

Violent crimes refer to instances of Homicides, Rape, Robberies or Assault. It is important to visualise crime statistics on a 'per capita' basis, as this normalises numbers for comparison. Looking at the map, we can see that St. Louis, Memphis and Detroit were the cities with highest crimes in 2015

Population Density impact on Assaults 2000-2015

The belief is that cities with greater populations will inevitabley have greater numbers of crimes (most prominent being assault), however the graph indicates that is not the case. Atlanta, Orlando and Detroit having high numbers of assault. Additionally, we can see an overall reducing trend in assualt numbers as we travel through the years

The significant drop in 1995

Looking at cities with the highest nominal (unadjusted) numbers of crime, we can see that crime numbers were significantly reduced between 1995-2005. This signifies the so called "Crime Drop". The crime drop or crime decline is a pattern observed in many countries whereby rates of many types of crime declined by 50% or more beginning in the mid 1990s. Further information can be found here: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crime_drop.

Population increased at a constant rate

It is interesting to see the the drop off in crime in 1995 is not linked to population changes over that period. Population has increased quite consistently, and New York soared between 1995-2000 yet showed a significant decrease in crime.

The most dangerous cities over time (1975-2015)

Atlanta, Baltimore, Detroit, Orlando and Tampa have show the highest numbers of over the 40 years between 1975-2015. From the steamgraph we can see that 1993 was the pinnacle of Crime, reporting the highest numbers before the "Crime Drop-Off" as mentioned above. In all 5 cities we can see reductions in crime even in more recent periods (2006 - 2015)
