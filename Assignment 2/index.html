The Journey from College to the NBA

An in-depth analysis of the origins and journeys of NBA players in the US

Where are NBA Players Born?

This maps shows the percentage of NBA players born in each state of the US. Of the over 3000 players in the NBA since 1950, around 2500 are born in America. Of those players, a staggered 11.18% and 9.49% are born in California and New York. These two states make up the birthplaces of one-fifth of the entire league historically!

This may be due to the strong basketball lineage that both these states possess. California is home to four NBA teams (LA Lakers, LA Clipper, Golden State Warriors and the Sacramento Kings) whilst New York also has two teams (NY Knicks and Brooklyn Nets). Having basketball heavily rooted in the culture of each state inspires local players to represent their hometown at the highest level of competition.

Which Colleges Draft the most NBA Players?

The University of Kentucky (Kentucky) and the University of California (California) have both drafted 84 NBA players since 1950. We learned in the previous chart that only 2.72% of all historic NBA players are both in Kentucky, yet the university is still able to produce the same volume of players in California.

This is because the Kentucky Wildcats are the most successful NCAA Division I (main college league) basketball program in history. The number one in all-time wins (2300), winning percentage (0.765) and have 5 NCAA Championships. Their prestigious head coach and development program attracts the best talent from across the state which is a winning combination to produce NBA players.

What type of players has the best chance to play in the NBA?

Statistically speaking, a player between 200-205 cm and weighting between 95-100 kgs has the best chance to play in the NBA.

Players of this build are in demand in the NBA because on offence they are tall and athletic enough to drive by and shoot over their opponents. On defence, they are heavy enough to stand their ground against larger players with the right length to contest/block shots.
